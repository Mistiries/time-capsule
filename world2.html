Yu-Gi-Oh Cards

It was a warm and sunny day in 2015 at my primary school. I was 8 years old. I had just confessed to two of my girl classmates that I had a crush on one of the boys. They were thrilled with this information and pushed me to confess. I was like 'noooo I'm scared', but they ran off to find the guy ANYWAYS.

The boy was very artistic and empathetic. I loved his art, and I loved his sarcastic humour. I had a crush on him from the start of primary school, in Year 2. I'd moved to the UK from Greece when I was 6, in 2013, and they place children by age rather than ability, so I started in an older year group, and he was one of the first people I remember.

I hid behind a tree waiting for him, and he turned up, alone, without the girls. I managed to get out what I wanted to say eventually, and he didn't say anything at first. Then he simply said, "That's cool." He took my hand, and we walked through the school playground together. He changed the subject and started talking about something else.

From that day, we were very good friends. We spent breaks together and drew together. The crush disappeared. I think I really just wanted to be friends with him.

It turns out he was gay, and I found out in high school from others. Looking back, it does kind of make sense. His friends were all the girls in the class; he was quite good with emotions and was way kinder than the other boys. I never spoke to him again after high school, but I like remembering the fun moments from primary school.

I think I felt it very intensely, but I just couldn't articulate it because I was young, and other people just saw it as a humongous crush, since there's not much vocabulary for it. I called it a crush, but what I actually wanted was closeness. The friendship was the point in the end, not the crush.
